Case 1:  Gender-Specific Stroke Mortality Rates 
  
Objective:
To assess stroke mortality rates by gender and identify the high-risk group to drive targeted prevention and healthcare interventions.


Strategy:
The data revealed that males have a significantly higher stroke mortality rate (81.90) compared to females (77.44). Public health officials can focus their campaigns on males, aiming to reduce mortality rates by implementing targeted gender-based interventions.


Positive Impact:
Targeted Gender-Based Health Programs
Focusing on the high-risk group (males) can lead to a potential reduction in stroke-related deaths, promoting early detection and risk mitigation through gender-specific healthcare programs. 

Negative Impact:
Risk of Overlooking Other Factors
Exclusively concentrating on gender might overlook other contributing factors such as socioeconomic status, lifestyle choices, or comorbidities that could play a significant role in stroke mortality.
