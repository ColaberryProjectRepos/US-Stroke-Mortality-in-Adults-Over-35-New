Methodology 4
Data Preparation 
The dataset underwent cleaning, transformation, and standardization to ensure consistency across all variables. Key features such as gender and race were encoded, and missing values were imputed to address data gaps. The data cleansing process emphasized ensuring high data quality for accurate analysis, and the refined data was stored in the Data Warehouse for future model development and analysis.


Model Selection 
Several regression models were tested for their predictive accuracy. After evaluating 107 models, the XGBoost Regressor emerged as the best performer due to its strong accuracy and efficiency in handling large datasets. The model explained 80% of the variance in stroke mortality rates, with optimized hyperparameters minimizing error margins, making it ideal for predicting outcomes and guiding public health interventions.


Model Development 
The XGBoost Regressor model was developed by tuning hyperparameters to improve prediction accuracy and reduce errors. The data was split into training and validation sets, with the model trained on 80% of the data. Key challenges like handling large datasets and optimizing processing were addressed, resulting in a model that predicts stroke mortality rates with high accuracy and minimized errors.
