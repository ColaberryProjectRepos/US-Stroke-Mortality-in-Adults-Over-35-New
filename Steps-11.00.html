7. Model Results Interpretation

The best model found was the XGBRegressor model.

R2: 0.8 - An R² of 0.8 shows that 80% of the variance in stroke mortality rates is explained by the model, indicating strong performance.

Mean Squared Error: 62.6 - An MSE of 62.6 reflects a reasonable average squared error, showing decent predictive accuracy.

Mean Absolute Error: 3.9 - An MAE of 3.9 means the model’s predictions are off by about 3.9 units on average, a good accuracy level.

Median Absolute Error: 1.1 - A MedAE of 1.1 shows that over half the prediction errors are less than 1.1 units, signaling solid model performance.

Variance: 0.8 - A variance score of 0.8 implies that the model maintains a high level of reliability and consistency in its predictions across different data points.
