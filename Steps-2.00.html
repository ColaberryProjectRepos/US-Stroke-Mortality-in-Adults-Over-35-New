Executive Summary

This case study delves into the persistent public health challenge of high stroke mortality rates across the U.S. By employing advanced predictive modeling and geographic risk analysis, the project uncovers significant patterns that impact stroke mortality across demographics and regions. With highly accurate predictions and minimized error margins, the insights from this project empower public health officials to proactively address stroke mortality, enabling targeted interventions that could reduce rates, save lives, and optimize healthcare resources for a healthier population.



Background
Stroke mortality is a pressing public health issue in the U.S., especially affecting adults over 35. Disparities in mortality rates across regions and demographic groups highlight an urgent need for targeted health interventions. These variations not only impact individual lives but also burden healthcare resources. This project analyzes extensive, age-standardized stroke mortality data segmented by location and demographics. By uncovering critical patterns, the findings aim to support public health strategies to reduce mortality rates and improve healthcare outcomes.


Problem Statement
Stroke mortality rates remain high among U.S. adults over 35, posing a serious public health challenge. Stroke is a leading cause of death in the U.S., claiming one life every 3.5 minutes. These high rates are particularly evident across certain regions and demographic groups, where disparities worsen the risk. This project seeks to analyze factors driving these patterns, using data-driven insights to inform targeted public health actions.



Objectives

The primary objective of this project is to identify high-risk areas and demographic groups with elevated stroke mortality rates among U.S. adults over 35. By leveraging comprehensive, age-standardized datasets and advanced analytics, the project aims to uncover significant patterns influencing stroke outcomes. These insights are intended to guide public health officials in implementing targeted, data-driven interventions, with the potential to reduce mortality rates and optimize healthcare resources.
