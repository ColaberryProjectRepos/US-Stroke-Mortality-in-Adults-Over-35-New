Case 2:  Regional Stroke Mortality Disparities
 
Objective:
To identify regions with the highest stroke mortality rates and focus healthcare resources on areas with elevated mortality.


Strategy: 
The analysis identified states like California (1109.47) and Delaware (1099.32) as having the highest stroke mortality rates. Public health officials can target these regions with focused healthcare programs, improving outcomes in high-risk areas.


Positive Impact:
Prioritizing High-Risk Regions
Targeting regions with elevated stroke mortality ensures healthcare resources are used efficiently, potentially lowering mortality rates by improving healthcare access and preventive measures. 

Negative Impact:
Potential Neglect of Smaller Regions
Concentrating only on the most affected regions may overlook emerging areas with increasing mortality rates. This could lead to a rise in stroke-related deaths in smaller or less prioritized regions due to insufficient interventions.
