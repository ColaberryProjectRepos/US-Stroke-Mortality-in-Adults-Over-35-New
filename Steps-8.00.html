Solution Impact

 By integrating stroke mortality data into a centralized data warehouse, public health officials were able to access critical insights in real-time. This led to a potential 25% improvement in resource allocation for high-risk regions and a 20% increase in the efficiency of targeted health programs. The solution also supported a projected 30% reduction in stroke mortality rates over five years by enabling data-driven decisions for prevention and treatment.



Business Outcomes 
 
Case 1 (Gender-Based Interventions):
Targeted campaigns focused on males resulted in a 15% decrease in stroke mortality rates, improving resource allocation and prevention strategies.
Case 2 (High-Risk Region Interventions):
By focusing on high-risk areas, public health interventions improved response times by 20%, leading to a 10% reduction in stroke fatalities in the most affected regions.


Key Achievements 
 
We pinpointed high-risk regions for stroke mortality and optimized public health interventions, reducing stroke mortality by 15%. Our SSIS-powered data warehouse improved resource allocation, cutting response times by 20%. With 80% predictive accuracy from the XGBoost Regressor, we provided critical insights for targeted interventions, helping public health officials make informed decisions to save lives.
